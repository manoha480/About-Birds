ROBIN

ROBINS are small to medium-sized birds known for their bright orange or red breast and melodious song. They belong to the family Turdidae and are found in various parts of the world, including North America, Europe, and Asia.

These birds are primarily insectivores but also eat fruits and berries. Robins are often seen hopping on lawns and gardens in search of food. They are known for their distinctive nesting behavior, building nests in trees or shrubs.

            
Types of Robins

            
    
                
  • American Robin - The most common species in North America, recognized by its red-orange breast.
    • 
                
  • European Robin - Known for its friendly nature and is often found in gardens across Europe.
    • 
                
  • Eastern Bluebird - A close relative with a similar appearance but a more vibrant blue coloration.
    • 
                
  • Red-breasted Robin - Found in parts of Asia and known for its striking red breast.
    • 
                
  • Rufous-tailed Robin - A smaller species with a rufous tail and a melodious song.
