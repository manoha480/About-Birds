Birds of Prey

Birds of Prey are carnivorous birds known for their sharp talons, hooked beaks, and excellent eyesight. They hunt and feed on animals like rodents, fish, and insects. Common types include eagles, hawks, owls, and falcons. These birds live in various habitats like forests, mountains, and cities. They play an important role in nature by controlling animal populations

Examples

  • Hawk

    • Hawks are medium-sized birds of prey known for their sharp vision and swift flight. They use their strong talons and beaks to catch and eat small animals like rodents and birds. Hawks are found in forests, open fields, and even cities. They are skilled hunters and often seen soaring high in the sky.

  • Owl

    • Owls are nocturnal birds of prey with excellent night vision and silent flight. They have large eyes, a flat face, and a sharp beak for hunting. Owls feed on small animals like mice, insects, and birds. They are often found in forests, farmlands, and even urban areas.
