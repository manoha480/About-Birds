OSTRICH

OSTRICHES are the largest living birds, native to Africa. They are flightless and known for their long necks and legs. Ostriches can run at high speeds, making them the fastest-running birds on land.

These birds primarily feed on plants, seeds, and insects. Ostriches are also unique for their large eyes and long eyelashes, which help protect them from sand and dust in their natural habitat.

            
Types of Ostriches

            
    
                
  • Common Ostrich - The most widespread species found in various African countries.
    • 
                
  • Southern Ostrich - Known for its distinctive black and white plumage.
    • 
                
  • Northern Ostrich - Found in North Africa, recognized by its lighter coloration.
    • 
                
  • Somali Ostrich - A subspecies with a unique appearance found in the Horn of Africa.
