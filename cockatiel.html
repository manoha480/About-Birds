COCKATIEL

Cockatiels are small to medium-sized parrots native to Australia. They are known for their friendly nature and distinctive crest on their head. Cockatiels come in various colors, including gray, white, and yellow.

These birds are social and enjoy interacting with their owners. They are herbivores, primarily feeding on seeds, fruits, and vegetables. Cockatiels are also known for their ability to whistle and mimic sounds, making them popular pets.

            
Types of Cockatiels

            
    
                
  • Normal Gray Cockatiel - The most common color, characterized by its gray plumage and orange cheek patches.
    • 
                
  • Lutino Cockatiel - A yellow mutation with red eyes, known for its striking appearance.
    • 
                
  • Cinnamon Cockatiel - Features a brownish hue instead of gray, with lighter cheek patches.
    • 
                
  • Whiteface Cockatiel - Lacks the typical yellow and orange coloration, resulting in a more subdued appearance.
    • 
                
  • Pearl Cockatiel - Exhibits a unique pattern of feathering that resembles pearls, adding to its beauty.
