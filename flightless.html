Flightless Birds

Flightless Birds are birds that cannot fly, even though they have wings. They have evolved to live on land or water and often have strong legs for walking or swimming. These birds usually live in places where there are few predators or on islands.

Examples

  • Ostrich

    • The ostrich is the largest and fastest-running bird, found in Africa.It cannot fly but has strong legs that help it run up to 70 km/h.Ostriches lay the biggest eggs and have excellent eyesight to spot danger.

  • Penguin

    • Penguins are flightless birds that live mostly in the Southern Hemisphere.They are excellent swimmers and use their flippers to move in water.Penguins have black and white feathers that help them stay warm in cold places.

  • Kiwi

    • The kiwi is a small, flightless bird native to New Zealand.It has a long beak with nostrils at the tip, used to find insects underground.Kiwis are shy and mostly active at night (nocturnal).
