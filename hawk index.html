HAWK

HAWKS are birds of prey known for their keen eyesight and powerful hunting skills. They belong to the family Accipitridae and are found in various habitats worldwide, including forests, grasslands, and deserts.

These birds primarily feed on small mammals, birds, and reptiles. Hawks are known for their soaring flight and are often seen gliding effortlessly in the sky.

            
Types of Hawks

            
    
                
  • Red-tailed Hawk - Recognized by its reddish-brown tail and is commonly found in North America.
    • 
                
  • Cooper's Hawk - A medium-sized hawk known for its agility and speed.
    • 
                
  • Sharp-shinned Hawk - The smallest hawk in North America, known for its sharp talons.
    • 
                
  • Swainson's Hawk - Known for its long migrations and is often seen in large flocks.
    • 
                
  • Ferruginous Hawk - The largest North American hawk, recognized by its rusty-colored plumage.
