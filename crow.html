CROW

Crows are highly intelligent, social birds that belong to the Corvidae family. They are found all over the world and are known for their adaptability, problem-solving skills, and communication.

Crows have shiny black feathers, strong beaks, and are often seen scavenging in urban and rural areas. They are omnivorous, meaning they eat both plants and animals.

Types of Crows


House Crow

    Found in India and Africa.

    Black body with grey neck.

American Crow

    Found in North America.

    Loud cawing, completely black.

Carrion Crow

    Found in Europe and Asia.

    Fully black and a scavenger.

Hooded Crow

    Found in Europe and Middle East.

    Grey body with black head and wings.

Fish Crow

    Found in USA.

    Smaller and has a nasal voice.

Jungle Crow

    Found in South Asia.

    Big in size with glossy feathers.

Pied Crow

    Found in Africa.

    Black and white body.

Rook

    Found in Europe and Asia.

    Has bare skin near the beak.
