Water Birds

Water Birds are birds that live near water bodies like lakes, rivers, and oceans. They have special adaptations that help them swim and find food in the water. Common water birds include ducks, swans, and kingfishers. They often have webbed feet and waterproof feathers to keep them dry.

    • Ducks are water birds with broad bills and webbed feet. They are often found in ponds and lakes. Ducks eat plants, insects, and small fish.
