BIRDS

Birds are a group of warm-blooded vertebrates constituting the class Aves, characterised by feathers, toothless beaked jaws, the laying of hard-shelled eggs, a high metabolic rate, a four-chambered heart, and a strong yet lightweight skeleton.

Birds are animals that have feathers, wings, and beaks. Most birds can fly, although some cannot. They lay eggs and take care of their young ones. Birds are found all over the world and come in many colors and sizes. Common birds include sparrows, pigeons, parrots, and eagles.

Birds are play an important role in nature by spreading seeds, controlling insects, and pollinating plants. With over 10,000 species, birds come in many sizes, colors, and behaviors, making them fascinating creatures to observe and study.

Birds are found in every part of the world, from the Arctic to the Antarctic. They live in forests, grasslands, deserts, and even cities. Birds are known for their beautiful songs and colorful feathers.
