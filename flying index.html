Flying Birds

Flying Birds are birds that can fly. They have wings and feathers that help them soar through the sky. Examples include sparrows, eagles, and parrots. Flying birds are known for their ability to migrate long distances, find food in the air, and escape from predators. They play a crucial role in ecosystems by pollinating plants and dispersing seeds.

Examples

  • Sparrow

    • The sparrow is a small, brown-colored bird found all over the world. It is known for its chirping sound and friendly nature. Sparrows often live near human houses and make nests in rooftops or trees. They eat grains, seeds, and small insects. Sparrows are social birds and usually move in groups.
