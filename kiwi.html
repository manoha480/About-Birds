KIWI

Kiwis are flightless birds native to New Zealand. They are unique for their long beaks and small wings, and they are nocturnal, primarily active at night. Kiwis are known for their brown, hair-like feathers and their sensitive nostrils at the end of their beaks.

These birds are omnivores, feeding on insects, worms, and fruits. Kiwis are also notable for being the only birds with nostrils located at the tip of their beaks, which helps them forage for food in the ground.

            
Types of Kiwis

            
    
                
  • North Island Kiwi - The most common species found in New Zealand's North Island.
    • 
                
  • South Island Kiwi - Found in the South Island, known for its larger size.
    • 
                
  • Little Spotted Kiwi - The smallest kiwi species, found on predator-free islands.
    • 
                
  • Great Spotted Kiwi - A larger species with a distinctive spotted plumage.
    • 
                
  • Okarito Kiwi - A rare species found only in the Okarito Forest of New Zealand.
