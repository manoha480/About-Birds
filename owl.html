OWL

OWLS are nocturnal birds of prey known for their distinctive round faces and large eyes. They belong to the order Strigiformes and are found in various habitats worldwide, including forests, grasslands, and deserts.

Owls are primarily carnivorous, feeding on small mammals, birds, and insects. They are known for their silent flight and exceptional hearing, which helps them locate prey in the dark.

            
Types of Owls

            
    
                
  • Barn Owl - Recognized by its heart-shaped face and is found in many parts of the world.
    • 
                
  • Great Horned Owl - Known for its tufted "horns" and is one of the most widespread owls in the Americas.
    • 
                
  • Snowy Owl - Famous for its striking white plumage and is native to the Arctic regions.
    • 
                
  • Eastern Screech Owl - A small owl known for its distinctive trilling call.
    • 
                
  • Burrowing Owl - Unique for its habit of living in burrows dug by other animals.
