SWAN

SWANS are large water birds known for their long necks and graceful appearance. They belong to the Anatidae family, which also includes ducks and geese. Swans are typically found in freshwater lakes, rivers, and ponds, where they feed on aquatic plants, insects, and small fish.

Swans are known for their strong pair bonds and often form lifelong partnerships. They are also known for their beautiful plumage, which can be white, black, or a combination of both, depending on the species. Swans are social birds and can often be seen in groups, especially during migration.

            
Types of Swans

            
    
                
  • Mute Swan - The most common swan species, known for its orange bill and white plumage.
    • 
                
  • Tundra Swan - Found in North America, recognized by its black bill and yellow patches.
    • 
                
  • Black Swan - Native to Australia, known for its striking black feathers and red bill.
    • 
                
  • Whooper Swan - Known for its loud trumpeting call and yellow facial markings.
    • 
                
  • Coscoroba Swan - A smaller swan species found in South America, with a distinctive white body and pink bill.
